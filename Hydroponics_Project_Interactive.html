🌱 DIY Hydroponics: The Wick System

Summer Holiday Assignment | Grades 8 & 9 | Science & Sustainability

The Challenge

Traditional agriculture requires vast amounts of fertile soil and water. As our urban spaces grow, we face a dual challenge: high plastic waste and limited space for greenery. How can we use "waste" materials to grow food efficiently without using a single handful of garden soil?

Materials Checklist:
  • Used 2L PET Bottle
  • Cotton cloth / Thick wick
  • Growth medium (Cocopeat/Perlite)
  • Cutter (Use with adult help)
  • Seeds (Basil, Spinach, or Methi)
  • Clean Water

DIY Procedure

  1. Cut the Bottle: Cut the PET bottle into two parts: a top funnel and a bottom reservoir.
  2. Make a Hole: Make a hole in the bottle cap.
  3. Attach Wick: Thread the cotton wick through the hole in the bottle cap.
  4. Assemble: Invert the top part into the bottom part like a funnel.
  5. Fill up: Fill the bottom with water and the top with your growth medium. Ensure the wick is buried in the medium and submerged in the water.
  6. Plant: Plant your seeds and place the system in a sunlit area.
